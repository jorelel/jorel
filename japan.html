Sapporo, Japan

 This place is one of my dreams when I was a kid. I wanna go to Japan to experience snows, culture, food, and festivals. Sapporo is the largest city on Japan's northern island of Hokkaido and serves as its capital. Known for its annual Sapporo Snow Festival, the city is famous for its impressive snow and ice sculptures. Sapporo is also celebrated for its beer, skiing resorts, and vibrant food scene, including dishes like Sapporo ramen and Genghis Khan (grilled mutton). The city has a unique blend of modern urban life and stunning natural landscapes, featuring parks, historic sites, and nearby mountains perfect for winter sports. Whether you're visiting for the snow festival, exploring the local cuisine, or enjoying the city's lively atmosphere, Sapporo offers a mix of tradition and modernity that’s uniquely Hokkaido.

Flight

 To get from Quezon City, Metro Manila to Sapporo, you'll need to fly to New Chitose Airport (CTS) in Hokkaido, Japan, and then take a limited express train to Sapporo Station.

Other places to visit

  • Kanazawa: "Little Kyoto" with Edo-era charm.
  • Fukuoka: Relaxed city, famous for food and Kyushu’s scenery.
  • Hakone: Hot springs, Mount Fuji views, and nature.
  • Himeji: Home to Japan’s iconic Himeji Castle.
  • Yokohama: Coastal city with modern and historic sights.
  • Kobe: Harbor views, Kobe beef, and earthquake memorial.
